17 years on the pole and I'm just getting started. What began as survival became revolution. I'm Reagan — performer, labor organizer, and your favorite stripper with a degree in Women & Gender Studies.

My work lives in the tension between the "janky" — the raw, DIY, imperfect reality of sex work — and the "glamour": the rhinestoned, spotlighted, unapologetic performance of femininity that pays my rent and fuels my art.

I'm a theater performer represented by Actors' Equity Association. I organize with grassroots collectives fighting for decriminalization. And in 2023, I helped make history.

The Fight That Changed Everything

"We do want to dance. We love it in there. We're fighting for safer working conditions."

In 2022, Reagan and her fellow dancers at Star Garden Topless Dive Bar in North Hollywood did something no strippers had ever done — they organized. After being fired for speaking up about unsafe conditions, they took their show to the sidewalk: picketing in costume, running runway shows outside the club, and building a movement that caught the attention of the entire labor world.

Fifteen months later, in May 2023, they won — a unanimous 17-0 vote. The Star Garden dancers became the only unionized strippers in the United States, represented by Actors' Equity Association.
